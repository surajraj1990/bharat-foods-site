The Science of Sacred Purity

Why the Purity of Lamp Oil Matters More Than You Think

Most commercially available lamp oils are paraffin-based — a petroleum derivative. When paraffin burns, it releases particulate matter (PM2.5), carbon monoxide, and volatile organic compounds (VOCs) including benzene and toluene. In an enclosed puja room, these compounds accumulate to levels that can exceed safe indoor air quality thresholds with just a few minutes of burning.

Ancient Indian texts did not describe paraffin — they didn't have it. What they described was til taila (sesame oil) and groundnut oil for lamps. The wisdom encoded in these prescriptions was empirical: these oils burn cleanly with minimal particulate output, are fully biodegradable, and produce a steady, clear flame.

Bharat Deepam Oil is formulated from pure vegetable oils specifically selected for clean, low-smoke combustion. No kerosene. No paraffin. No petrochemical derivatives. Just the oil the tradition always intended.
