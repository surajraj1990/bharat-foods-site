Purity & Chemistry

Why Your Groundnut Oil Should Smell Like Groundnuts

Walk into any Indian kitchen where genuine cold-pressed groundnut oil is being used, and you'll smell it immediately — a warm, distinctive nutty fragrance that commercially refined oil simply cannot produce. That aroma isn't an additive; it's the volatile aromatic compounds preserved because we never exposed the oil to the deodorisation process.

Refined peanut oil is deliberately stripped of its natural aroma because the industrial refining process introduces off-flavours from oxidation at high temperatures. Deodorisation removes these, but also removes the natural Vitamin E (tocopherols) that protect the oil and your cells.

Our cold-pressed groundnut oil is naturally stable because of its inherent Vitamin E content — no synthetic preservatives needed, and no flavour masking required.

Nutrition & Uses

The Heart-Healthy All-Purpose Oil

Groundnut oil has one of the most balanced fatty acid profiles of any cooking oil — high in heart-healthy monounsaturated fats (MUFA) and moderate in polyunsaturated fats (PUFA), with enough stability for high-heat cooking and nutritional richness for raw use in dressings.

Its high Vitamin E content makes cold-pressed groundnut oil naturally shelf-stable and a consistent source of this fat-soluble antioxidant in the diet.

The smoke point of ~232°C makes it suitable for virtually all Indian cooking methods: deep frying, sautéing, tempering, and slow cooking.

In Your Kitchen

How to Use Cold-Pressed Groundnut Oil

🍳

Daily Frying & Sautéing

With its high smoke point and mild flavour, groundnut oil is the default choice for daily Indian cooking. It doesn't overpower spices or main ingredients.

🍞

Baking & Roasting

The neutral flavour profile makes it exceptional for baking — from rotis to cakes. It imparts moisture without competing with other flavours.

🥗

Dressings & Marinades

Used cold, the naturally nutty character of cold-pressed groundnut oil adds depth to salad dressings and marinades that refined oil cannot match.
