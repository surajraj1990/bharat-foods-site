From Seed to Shelf

The Cold-Pressed Difference

We don't manufacture oil. We liberate it — from seed to bottle, the way it has been done in India for thousands of years.

How We Do It

The Wooden Ghani Process

Four steps. Zero heat above 49°C. Zero chemical solvents. Every nutrient intact from the moment the seed meets the press to the moment the oil reaches your kitchen.

🌾

Seed Selection & Sourcing

Every oil begins with the seed — and the seed's origin defines everything that follows. We source directly from farming partners who cultivate without synthetic pesticide overuse, giving us seeds with higher native oil content and cleaner fatty acid profiles.

Mustard seeds are sourced from Rajasthan and Punjab. Groundnuts from Gujarat. Sesame from Madhya Pradesh and Rajasthan. Each region's terroir expresses itself in the final oil — this is why cold-pressed oils from different sources taste noticeably different.

Seeds are cleaned, sorted, and checked for moisture content before pressing. Excess moisture in seeds causes oxidative degradation during pressing — a quality control step most producers skip.
⚙️

The Wooden Ghani Press

The wooden ghani (also called kolhu) is a slow-rotation pestle-and-mortar press driven by a central wooden or stone pestle. It applies gentle, sustained mechanical pressure to the seeds, coaxing the oil out through friction and compression — never through chemical extraction or heat application.

The rotation speed is deliberately kept slow — typically 6–8 RPM — to prevent friction-generated heat from rising above 49°C. Above this threshold, volatile aroma compounds begin to evaporate and heat-sensitive fatty acids begin to degrade. The ghani's slow pace is not inefficiency — it is precision.

Industrial expeller presses run at 60–90 RPM and generate internal temperatures of 85–95°C, destroying the very compounds that make cold-pressed oils nutritionally superior.
🫙

Natural Gravity Filtration

Freshly pressed oil is a suspension of oil, water, and fine seed particles. We allow this to settle naturally over 24–48 hours through gravity filtration alone — no centrifuge, no filter press, no chemical clarification agents.

The settled oil is then passed through clean, food-grade cloth filters to remove remaining particulates. This process preserves the natural waxes, phospholipids, and trace compounds that commercial filtration strips away — compounds that contribute to shelf stability and nutritional depth.

You may notice a natural, fine sediment at the bottom of your Bharat Oils bottle. This is not contamination — it is the signature of an unfiltered, natural product. Swirl gently before use.
📦

Batch Testing & Bottling

Every batch is tested before bottling: Free Fatty Acid (FFA) content, Peroxide Value (PV), and specific adulteration tests per FSSAI guidelines. Mustard oil is tested for argemone contamination. Groundnut oil is tested for mineral oil adulteration. Sesame oil is subjected to the Bellier test.

Bottles are filled in a clean environment and sealed immediately to minimise oxygen exposure. We use dark-tinted glass and food-grade HDPE bottles to protect the oil from light-induced oxidation — the primary enemy of shelf stability in cold-pressed oils.

Store your Bharat Oils bottle in a cool, dark place. Refrigeration extends shelf life but may cause natural cloudiness — this resolves at room temperature and does not indicate spoilage.

The Science

What Happens to Oil During Refining?

Commercial oil refining is a multi-stage industrial process designed to produce a shelf-stable, flavour-neutral, visually consistent product. Each stage removes something the original seed put in.

🌿

Cold-Pressed Oil (Bharat Oils)

✅
Natural Vitamins Intact: Vitamin E (tocopherols) fully preserved — acts as natural antioxidant and extends shelf life.
✅
Polyphenols Present: Sesamol, resveratrol, lignans — bioactive compounds with anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
✅
Natural Aroma: Volatile compounds that define the authentic flavour profile of each oil — untouched by deodorisation.
✅
Natural Colour: Carotenoids and chlorophylls give the oil its characteristic hue — not synthetic dyes added back after bleaching.
✅
Phospholipids Retained: Natural emulsifiers that aid digestion and contribute to cellular membrane health.
✅
Zero Solvent Residue: No hexane or chemical extraction solvents involved at any stage.
🏭

Commercially Refined Oil

❌
Vitamins Destroyed: High-temperature deodorisation (240–270°C) destroys up to 80% of natural Vitamin E content.
❌
Polyphenols Removed: Alkali refining (neutralisation) removes natural polyphenols along with free fatty acids.
❌
Aroma Stripped: Steam deodorisation specifically eliminates all volatile aroma compounds — the oil becomes flavourless by design.
❌
Bleached: Bleaching clay removes colour-causing compounds — and along with them, many beneficial carotenoids.
❌
Phospholipids Removed: Degumming strips phospholipids to improve clarity and extend shelf life.
❌
Hexane Extracted: Solvent extraction using hexane (a petroleum derivative) maximises yield — trace residues remain.

Adulterant Awareness

How to Test for Argemone Oil at Home

Argemone mexicana oil contamination in mustard oil has caused mass poisonings in India — most notably the 1998 Delhi epidemic that killed 65 people and hospitalised thousands. Here's how to verify your oil at home before you trust it.

The Nitric Acid Home Test

This test detects sanguinarine, the primary toxic alkaloid in argemone oil. It requires concentrated nitric acid — handle with extreme care and adult supervision only.

1
Take 5 ml of the oil to be tested in a clean glass test tube or small glass jar.
2
Add 5 ml of concentrated nitric acid carefully along the side of the glass — do not shake or mix.
3
Observe the interface between the oil and acid layers. Wait 1–2 minutes without disturbing.
4
Result — Pure: The acid layer remains colourless or turns faint yellow. Result — Adulterated: The acid layer turns orange or red — indicating sanguinarine from argemone oil.

Bharat Oils Mustard Oil passes this test every batch. We test at source so you don't have to — but we believe an informed customer is the best safeguard for the entire supply chain.

Farm to Bottle

Where Our Seeds Come From

The quality of cold-pressed oil is only as good as the seed it begins with. We work directly with regional farming partners — no brokers, no commodity markets.

🌼

Mustard — Rajasthan & Punjab

The Sarson belt of North India produces mustard with exceptionally high glucosinolate content — the compound responsible for the characteristic pungency and antimicrobial properties of pure mustard oil.

🥜

Groundnut — Saurashtra, Gujarat

Gujarat's Saurashtra region is India's largest groundnut producing belt. The semi-arid red soil produces groundnuts with a naturally high oleic acid ratio — ideal for cold-pressed oil stability.

✨

Sesame — Madhya Pradesh

MP's black sesame and white sesame varieties are among the richest in sesamin and sesamolin content in India. The region's traditional cultivation methods align closely with our quality requirements.
