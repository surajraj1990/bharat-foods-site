Smoke Point Guide

At what temperature each Bharat Oil begins to smoke — and what cooking methods that suits.

OilSmoke PointBest For
🥜 Groundnut~180°CDeep frying, high-heat
🌼 Mustard~160°CTempering, frying (after smoking)
🥥 Coconut~175°CSautéing, medium heat
✨ Sesame~160°CLow heat, finishing drizzle

Note: Cold-pressed oils have slightly lower smoke points than refined — but their antioxidant content makes them more heat-stable at those temperatures than their smoke point alone suggests.

Fatty Acid Profiles

The fat composition that determines nutritional character, flavour, and heat stability.

OilMUFAPUFASaturated
🥜 Groundnut46%32%17%
🌼 Mustard60%21%12%
🥥 Coconut6%2%92%
✨ Sesame40%42%14%

MUFA = monounsaturated (heart-protective). PUFA = polyunsaturated (essential fatty acids). Coconut oil's saturated fats are primarily MCTs — metabolised differently to animal saturated fats.

Oil Substitution Guide

What to use when a recipe calls for an oil you don't have — and what the trade-off is.

Refined vegetable oil → → Groundnut Oil Direct swap. Same heat stability, better flavour, better nutrition.
Olive oil (cooking) → → Groundnut Oil Better heat stability. More neutral flavour for Indian cooking.
Toasted sesame oil → → Sesame Oil Cold-pressed sesame oil has deeper flavour. Use slightly less.
Refined coconut oil → → Coconut Oil Same cooking properties. Cold-pressed version retains coconut aroma.
✅

Purity Checklist

How to verify that an oil is genuinely cold-pressed and unadulterated — before you buy it and after.

✓
Natural sediment at the bottom — fine particles settle in cold-pressed oil. This is a good sign, not a defect.
✓
Distinctive natural smell — mustard oil should be pungent, sesame should be aromatic, coconut should smell coconut-y.
✓
Cloudiness in cold weather — groundnut and coconut oil will cloud or solidify below certain temperatures. This is natural and reversible.
✗
Perfectly clear and odourless — if an oil has no smell and perfect clarity, it has been refined and deodorised. Not cold-pressed.
✗
Labels saying "natural flavour added" — genuine cold-pressed oil needs no added flavour. This phrase signals deodorised oil with synthetic aroma re-added.
